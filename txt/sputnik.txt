The Soviet Union launched Sputnik 1, the first artificial satellite, into orbit on October 4, 1957. This event marked the beginning of the space age and the start of the Space Race between the United States and the Soviet Union. Sputnik's successful launch demonstrated that space exploration was possible and led to significant advancements in science and technology, spurring the development of space programs around the world. It also prompted the United States to establish NASA in 1958.
